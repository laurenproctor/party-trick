This document defines the complete brand system — principles, color, type, components, expression, voice — for every marketing surface (landing, product, about, pricing, FAQ). PRINCIPLES

READ FIRST

Six rules that define everything else. The system is the spine; the brand is what gets applied on top. break only on purpose →

SYSTEM-FIRST.
FEELING-LAST.

The page reads like a manual. Mono everywhere. Brackets, slashes, prompts. Then we apply the feeling — a sticker, a scrawl, a strikethrough — onto the system.

HARD
EDGES.

Borders are 2px solid black. No rounded corners. No drop shadows except as offset blocks (Memphis-style). Every container has a clear edge.

2 SIGNALS
+ MARK.

Off-white paper. Ink black. Bright green as primary signal. Electric blue for pop. Acid-lime highlighter for annotations. Nothing else.

RUN-ON
NAVIGATION.

Links live inside sentences. Reading the nav is reading the brand. No icon-only menus. No "Home / About / Pricing" trios.

STICKER
OVERLAY.

Brand applies on top of the layout — taped corners, rotated stamps, hand annotations. The system is the page; the brand is the sticker.

[ 06 ]

DON'T BE CUTE.

Anti-references: pastels, kawaii, hand-drawn flowers, "girlboss" cursive on pink. Party Trick is messy, late, sexy, specific. Not adorable.

VOICE

HOW IT SOUNDS · TONE · COPY

Drunk art-school friend with good instincts, not a polished software product. Funny, slightly mean, culturally literate, fast, specific. stands in the room. takes notes.

[ ☠ RULE ZERO ]
// THE ONE THING WE NEVER SAY
Never name the technology.

Party Trick is not an AI tool, an AI portrait generator, AI-powered, or a machine learning product. It's a witness. A souvenir engine. A bad friend with a sketchpad. We are Party Trick — a character with taste and grudges — not a model behind a button.

[ B · VOICE PRINCIPLES — IN PRACTICE ]
FOUR DIALS, ALWAYS ON.

SPECIFIC BEATS GENERIC.

If you can swap the noun for any other product, it's too generic. Name the outfit, the drink, the exact facial expression.

BAD

Turn your party moments into funny AI art.

BETTER

Upload the voice note. We'll identify the vibe, the outfit, the social damage, and the exact facial expression that ruined the night.

MEAN — NOT CRUEL.

Roast the situation. Never the person. The reader should laugh, not flinch.

BAD

You looked insane.

BETTER

You had the posture of someone explaining natural wine to a captive audience.

PARTY TRICK HAS
OPINIONS.

Never neutral. Party Trick has taste, grudges, and a minor drinking problem. Write every line like it's the funniest person at the table — never like a tool announcing what it just did.

BAD

BETTER
